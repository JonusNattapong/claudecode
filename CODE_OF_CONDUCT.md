# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as maintainers, contributors, and community members pledge to make participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, respectful, inclusive, and professional community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

* Being respectful of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
* Giving and accepting constructive feedback
* Accepting responsibility for mistakes
* Focusing on what is best for the project and its users
* Communicating with patience and clarity

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

* Sexualized language, imagery, attention, or advances of any kind
* Trolling, insults, derogatory comments, or personal attacks
* Public or private harassment
* Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission
* Threats, intimidation, or sustained disruption
* Other conduct that could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

* Issues
* Pull requests
* Discussions
* Code review
* Documentation
* Community chat or support channels
* Any other space managed by the project maintainers

It also applies when someone represents the project in public, such as through an official project account, project email address, release announcement, or maintainer role.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may remove, edit, reject, or lock comments, commits, code, issues, pull requests, discussions, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may also temporarily or permanently ban contributors or participants for behavior they consider inappropriate, threatening, offensive, harmful, or disruptive.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will consider the severity, impact, context, and pattern of behavior when deciding enforcement actions.

### 1. Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or behavior considered unprofessional or unwelcome.

**Consequence:** A private written warning from maintainers explaining the issue and expected behavior. A public apology may be requested when appropriate.

### 2. Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or repeated behavior.

**Consequence:** A formal warning with conditions for continued participation. The person may be asked to avoid interaction with specific people or topics for a defined period.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards or sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from project spaces for a defined period. During this period, the person may not interact publicly or privately with the project community or maintainers about project matters.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** A pattern of violations, harassment, threats, aggression, or conduct that makes the project unsafe or unproductive.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from project spaces.
